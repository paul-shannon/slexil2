The First Three Lines of Dante's Inferno: A Demonstration of SLEXIL

ELAN from the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics is a standard and well-supported desktop application for annotating audio and video. SLEXIL creates an interactive web page from ELAN's XML output. Text scrolls as the recording plays. Single line mode is invoked by clicking on any line number button. Other controls are provided - for playback speed, font size and tier visibility.

Our python code performs the transformation from ELAN. Alternatively the data may be entered in a simple YAML file.

Here we captured Roberto Benigni's audio from here. Translation and morphemic analysis courtesy of the University of Alberta Department of Linguistics.

More elaborate presentations with open-ended annotation of transcriptions and morphemes are also possible. For more information, contact Paul Shannon.
